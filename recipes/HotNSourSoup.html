HotNSour Soup

Recipe

Hot and sour soup is a flavorful Chinese soup known for its balanced mix of spicy, tangy, and savory flavors. The key ingredients include mushrooms, tofu, and bamboo shoots, all simmered in a broth that is both spicy and sour, thanks to the addition of rice vinegar and chili garlic sauce. This soup is often enjoyed as a starter in Chinese cuisine and is loved for its complex taste and comforting warmth.

This soup is thickened with cornstarch, which gives it a slightly viscous texture that helps to carry the flavors of the broth. The addition of beaten egg adds a silky texture and protein, making it a wholesome dish. Green onions and optional fresh cilantro provide a fresh contrast to the rich, hearty soup. Hot and sour soup is not only delicious but also simple to prepare, making it a popular choice for both home cooks and restaurant chefs alike

Ingredients

  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake, wood ear, or button)
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots, julienned
  • 1/2 cup firm tofu, cut into small cubes
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili garlic sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (slurry)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add vegetables and tofu: Add the sliced mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and tofu to the boiling broth. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  3. Season the soup: Stir in the rice vinegar, soy sauce, and chili garlic sauce. Let the soup continue to simmer for another 5 minutes.
  4. Thicken the soup Slowly add the cornstarch slurry to the pot while continuously stirring until the soup thickens.
  5. Add egg: Pour the lightly beaten egg into the soup in a thin stream, stirring gently to create egg ribbons.
  6. Season to taste: Add the ground white pepper and sesame oil, adjusting the seasoning as necessary.
  7. Finish the soup: Remove the soup from heat and stir in the chopped green onions.
  8. Serve: Ladle the hot and sour soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
